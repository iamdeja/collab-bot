Play fair, play fun!

  1. 1.Play fair.

    The simplest and most immutable of rules. For everyone to have fun, please don't participate in any form of cheating. This includes but is not limited to: using engines, advising or being advised by another player and using opening books or assisted analysis.

  2. 2.Respect the format.

    When setting up your chess game, please make sure to use the settings previously agreed upon, or dictated by the tournament.

  3. 3.Respect your opponent.

    We know, it can be as frustrating to be defeated as it can be satisfying to win. Whatever happens, please respect your opponent so that both of you can have fun. This also means you shouldn't purposefully sabotage the game for your opponent.

  4. 4.Respect the rules of chess.

    Woohoo, digitial chess! Illegal moves can't be made by default. However other aspects such as asking for a takeback or needing more time can, but don't have to be satisfied by your opponent. You're free to offer and ask, but please don't get upset should the other party decline, as that is their right.

  5. 5.Play under your own account.

    When playing matches, please use your own account and do not give others access to it either. We discourage playing as / against anonymous accounts, but if that happens, you have the responsibility of identifying your opponent.

  6. 6.Turn in the results.

    After finishing your games, please send the links to the games to a member of the team. This should be done regardless of what your opponent tells you. Better both of you than neither.

  7. 7.Plan ahead.

    Please plan ahead of time when preparing for your match. If something urgent comes up during the match, there is no way to stop the clocks. Your opponent can decide to draft the game, but isn't required to, so you cannot dispute cancelled matches (be mindful of misclicks).

  8. 8.Have fun.

    Look, we just want all of you to have fun and these rules are here not to punish you, but to help create a positive and equal atmosphere. This way, both newcomers and avid players alike can gain something from these events and the wonderful game that is chess.
